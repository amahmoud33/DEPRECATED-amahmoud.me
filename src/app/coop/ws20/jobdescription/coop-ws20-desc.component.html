Overview and Main Tasks

Unitron Web Developers work in a team alongside other developers to implement web-based solutions using cutting-edge technologies. Unitron developers work under a strong and well thought out development architecture and process. Unitron also has a team of seasoned engineers that are committed to mentoring enable the growth of co-op students. Typically, Unitron developers work in an agile, collaborative and transparent working environment.

Main tasks include:

  • Developing and analyzing web-based software solutions for the hearing aid markets
  • Debugging and resolving software issues
  • Participating in agile project execution; daily stand ups, code reviews and product demos
  • Work closely with other teams such as marketing to ensure the final product adheres to the set requirements

Required Skills

As a Unitron Web Developer you are expected to bring strong technical skills and show competence in web development technologies. Experience with Node.js, the latest version of Angular, and Typescript is required for frontend user interface development. Experience with C#, .NET framework, SQL server, and CosmosDB is required for backend web service development.

My Day-to-Day Experience

As mentioned in my previous co-op report, I quickly learned about the importance of trust. On my first day Ara Talaslian, VP of Research and Development at Unitron, stressed the value trust. He mentioned that in order to be successful in any field you must be reliable, timely, and productive. In turn, this builds trust with other team members and allows your role to expand. In my experience, as I continued to work and produce code reliably during my term, I was entrusted with riskier tasks which enabled my growth as a developer.

During everyday work, I spent a substantial amount of time work closely with marketing team to ensure that requirements are met and to communicate any potential changes in the requirements. In addition, I spent a substantial amount of time work closely with the SQA team to ensure that software is easily testable and bug free. Moreover, as an agile team, we had stand-ups, web sprint meetings, and demo days where we showcased the latest development of the newest features.
