Overview and Main Tasks

Unitron aims to develop, design, manufacture and distribute solutions that push the limits of technology and redefine the future of the hearing aid industry. Unitron developers work under a strong and well thought out development architecture and process. Unitron also has a team of seasoned engineers that are committed to mentoring enable the growth of co-op students. Typically, Unitron developers work in an agile, collaborative and transparent working environment.

Main tasks include:

  • Develop and analyze software solutions for hearing aid market applications, hearing aid manufacturing and internal sales and marketing support
  • Debugging and resolving software issues
  • Participating in agile project execution; daily stand ups, code reviews and product demos
  • Work closely with other teams such as marketing to ensure the final product adheres to the set requirements
  • Create design documents as required to ensure that requirements are implemented to meet customer's expectations

Required Skills

As a Unitron Software Developer you are expected to bring strong technical skills with competence in the relevant technologies utilized at Unitron. These technologies include .NET framework, SQL server, Windows Presentation Foundation (WPF), Teamcity and more! Finally, Unitron developers need to be able to learn and adapt quickly to new technologies.

As a returning co-op I worked on a completely brand-new technology stack as I was no longer on the web team but instead working with the fitting software team. Although some of the technologies were different, they were still based on C#, so I picked them up with relative ease. In addition, Unitron frequently provides workshops, online resources, and mentorship when required to ensure that employees are competent.

My Day-to-Day Experience

Every term I have worked in Unitron has reiterated the importance of trust which is a focal point of being a successful team. Ara Talaslian, VP of Research and Development at Unitron, stressed the value trust. He mentioned that in order to be successful in any field you must be reliable, timely, and productive. In turn, this builds trust with other team members and allows your role to expand.

Each day I worked on my assigned projects and user stories and I spent a fair amount of time working closely with other developers to find solutions. I was provided the opportunity to work on many different projects so everyday was something new! I also worked with the SQA team to ensure that software is easily testable and bug free. Moreover, as an agile team, we had stand-ups, web sprint meetings, and demo days where we showcased the latest development of the newest features.
