Unitron's History

Unitron was founded in 1964 in Newfoundland, Canada by partners Fred Stork, Rolf Strothmann, and Rolf Dohmer. Unitron’s mission is to provide top of the line hearing aid technology to improve the lives of those who have hearing loss. Unitron's eternal mission has been providing an amazing user experience thats highly customizable. Throughout the 1960s-1980s, Unitron became a leader in Behind the Ear (BTE) hearing aids for people with severe to profound hearing loss.

As hearing aids transitioned from analogue to digital, Unitron did not stop innovating. As the 1990s began, Unitron started providing user-friendly and intuitive fitting software. This fitting software allowed advanced adjustments to be done to the digital hearing aids.

In the early 2000s, Unitron was acquired by Phonak AG which eventually was transitioned into Sonova. Unitron continued growing and eventually introduced the FLEX™ ecosystem and FLEX:TRIAL™ which allowed potential users to try the latest technologies before purchase.

More recently Unitron has positioned it self to grow as its own entity by taking risks on innovation projects and pushing its FLEX ecosystem. In 2020 innovations continued with the release of the Discover Next platform which promised improved performance especially when it comes to speech. Discover Next helps people understand who is talking, where the voices are coming from, what intonation they’re using, and what their underlying emotions are.

Unitron's Discover Next platform aids the user in understanding the emotional component of their conversations.

Unitron's Mission and Culture

As mentioned above, Unitron’s mission is to provide the best experience to its users. This person-centric culture is embedded in the entirety of the company, from top to bottom. Unitron’s “Love the Experience” tagline refers to a core culture within Unitron which involves providing the best emotional experience which leads to happier customers. Unitron’s hearing aids are not just a product. In 2020 this idea was manifested with the Discover Next platform.

Unitron’s culture extends to its many employees with various benefits. At Unitron, they allow employees to work within a specific range of hours to either leave earlier or come in later than usual. In addition, they allow employees to work extra hours during the week to leave earlier on another day. These policies add extra flexibility to the workplace and increase morale.

Finally, there are various internal committees (in addition to HR) that ensure a positive internal culture. The first is the Social Committee, they work closely with HR to run various events for employees such as lunches and sporting events. The events are accessible, cheap, and there is no pressure to participate if you are not interested. Unitron also has the Unitron Community Connection which regularly run charity events that give employees an opportunity to contribute to something positive and give back to the community.

Unitron continues to support The Hear the World (HTW) Foundation and the Lions Foundation of Canada Dog Guides

Unitron's Software Technology

Unitron offers Truefit™, a desktop hearing aid fitting software that allows hearing healthcare providers to make changes to the hearing aid digitally. In addition to this, Unitron offers technology levels, were by using Truefit™ a user can easily upgrade a hearing aid by adding more programs. Hearing aid programs are profiles which adjust various frequencies to improve hearing based on the environment (i.e. Driving program or TV program). Truefit™ is built using WPF technology.

In addition to Truefit™ there is the Web side of development. The Web side houses the production databases (SQL, CosmosDB, MongoDB, and more) and various front-end/back-end features that target hearing healthcare provides and users such as Patient Insights or FLEX:TRACKER™. Majority of web technology utilizes the .NET framework and the user interfaces are built using Angular. In 2019 Unitron introduced a Feedback Response feature which utilizes Microsoft's service bus as well Google's FCM to send notifications that inform the user of issues specific to their hearing aid. In 2020 Unitron added additional functionality to the Patient Insights which provides both patients and doctors real-time hearing aid use information to help doctors make any necessary adjustments.

Finally, Unitron has been pushing its software technology directly to its consumers through a mobile app. The mobile developers produce apps that pair directly with the hearing aids to provide the hearing aid user with troubleshooting steps, customization options and much more.

Unitron's Truefit™ fitting software provides Hearing Care Professionals (HCPs) the ability to efficiently and accurately setup their patients brand new hearing aid to provide them the best experience possible!
