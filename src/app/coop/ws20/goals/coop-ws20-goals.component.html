Improving Inquiry and Analysis Skills by Working with a New Codebase and Technologies

The W20/S20 co-op term was vastly different for me as I was no longer exclusively working on the web team as I had done so in my previous terms. Although I had some understanding of the Truefit™ software I had next to no experience with its codebase. As a result, improving my inquiry and analysis skills was critical in order for me to continue being a productive and efficient developer. In addition, I have always believed that there are certain skills such as inquiry and analysis skills that are the foundation of any strong developer.

During this co-op term I worked on a plethora of new projects that were built on top of existing legacy solutions. A major project I was involved in was a data aggregation solution in which a web portal was created that collected, cleaned, formatted and exported Sonova’s hearing aid data to an easy to navigate web application. This web application provides human digestible data about Sonova’s hearing aid products across all its major brands (including Unitron) to our in-house developers, SQA team, and product managers. While working on this project I was exposed to a variety of databases, processes, and data structures. I spent a lot of my time as a developer analyzing the data whether it was in the form of an enum value, hex value or a BLOB. I also developed my inquiry and analysis skills further by interpreting the provided requirements and inquiring about any requirements that were vague or non-technical.

In addition, I worked on a variety of user stories throughout the co-op term from user interface changes and translations to creating emulation of hearing aids. When tackling these user stories, I utilized my inquiry and analysis skills in a similar matter by dealing with requirements directly. Due to the sheer number of different types of user stories I worked on during this term I feel that my inquiry and analysis skills improved greatly.

Hearing aid data is typically stored in BLOBs and requires additional resources to analyze its value

Improving my Breath and Depth of .NET and WPF Technologies

As a developer, I am always looking to increase my knowledge about various technologies and as such every co-op term I set a goal to increase my breadth and depth of knowledge. This term I was given a fantastic opportunity to do as I was no longer exclusively working with the web team. I was now a general software developer which meant that I was required to learn new technologies and increase my depth of knowledge in technologies I worked with previously.

As mentioned, in my previous co-op terms I was strictly a web developer but in my last co-op term I was given the opportunity to work on Unitron’s flagship fitting software Truefit™. The fitting software has a WPF frontend with a .NET backend for all the platform code. I had never worked on a WPF client and as a result I spent time expanding my breadth of knowledge by learning the basics of the technology. As for .NET I had experience with it in my previous co-op terms on the web team as I directly worked with the .NET and .NET core frameworks for many of our APIs. However, creating a platform for a desktop application is wildly different from creating an API and as a result I expanded my depth of knowledge in .NET by working in a different arena.

In addition, I worked on a data aggregation project in which introduced me to a new open-source business intelligence server – Metabase. Working with Metabase increased my depth of knowledge in setting up and working with various databases and connecting them securely and efficiently to a remote server. In addition, I worked with the ETL (extract, transform, and load) model for the very first time which increased my breadth of knowledge. I even increased my breadth of knowledge when it came to various business metrics that were built into Metabase’s functionality. Upon reflection, I believe that I successfully expanded my breadth and depth of knowledge which makes me very happy.

Integrative Communication when Working with Multiple Teams

In multiple terms I have specified oral and written communication as a skill I would like to improve. Being a part of a team requires effective communication to ensure there are no assumptions and no conflicts between team members’ work. However, aside from basic oral and written communication skills, integrative communication also plays a critical role in the efficiency and performance of a team. Integrative communication involves cross-team adaption in communication strategy and without this teams can be fragmented.

As mentioned, I was no longer working solely on the web team, instead I was directly working alongside the web, fitting software, hearing aid software and software quality assurance teams. Working alongside so many different teams that all have their own processes, jargon, and communication channels provided me with a great opportunity to develop my communication skills and adapt it accordingly. My goal was to improve my communication in multi team meetings by being able to efficiently explain technical details at an appropriate level.

At times I got into too much detail and utilized jargon that was not inclusive to certain meeting members such as product management. I believe that over time I became much more self-aware in this sense and I tried my best to provide high-level overviews that everyone in the meetings can understand. Another aspect I improved on was my initiative in getting addition details and assistance from other team members. As I spent time working with each team, I began understanding who to contact for each type of inquiry and how much time to allocate for me working on the problem alone before going to that person.

Our standups included developers, SQA testers, and product management which meant that explanations of tasks needed to use inclusive language and limited jargon

Improving Problem Solving Skills to Build On-Top of Existing Solutions

For a software developer, problem solving skills are a foundation and is essential in everyday work. Stronger problem solving skills result in more robust solutions that do not cause issues over time. Each co-op term I try to develop my problem solving skills and this term was no different. Again, with me no longer exclusively working with the web team I was presented with a number of new problems types.

From working on a data aggregation project all the way to UI user stories I have solved so many different types of problems. These experiences not only helped improve my breadth and depth of knowledge but also in turn improved my problem solving as I am now much more proficient at dealing with specific types of problems and I have created strategies in doing so.

A strong example was another project I worked on which was a country availability tool which exported country availabilities for each hearing aid and where it was sold. Initially the tool divided each exported data set by country and each data set had all major brands within it. Eventually a product manager requested that the data sets also be divided by brand. I had previous experience in solving this division by brand problem when I was working on the data aggregation tool. I knew exactly which enums to use and what values to corresponded to each brand and its licensors. As such, I was able to leverage this previous experience to devise a similarly structured solution to the problem. As mentioned, I believe that growing my experiences in different types of problems have improved my problem solving tremendously.

Civic Knowledge & Engagement

At Sonova we have a lot of great events and programs such as dog walks, sports, and fundraisers. As such, I looked to be more engaged in the various social activities provided and to participate as much as possible. People at Unitron are extremely inclusive so it was super easy to sign up for any event regardless of ability.

My first event I signed up for was the Euchre tournament which is a card game that I was not familiar with. However, people took time out of their day to explain the rules and I found a partner. Although we did not win any games, we had lots of fun and I connected more with my co-workers.

Unfortunately, with the eventual strike of COVID-19 a lot of other events such as the Mario Kart tournament and BBQs were cancelled. However, our social committee quickly adapted and ran various online events, with some even contributing to charities to relief COVID-19 victims! Being a part of these events gave me a feeling of pride and fulfillment. I am happy to say that I believe I was involved in many civic and social events and that I became more knowledgeable about Sonova and Unitron as a whole.
