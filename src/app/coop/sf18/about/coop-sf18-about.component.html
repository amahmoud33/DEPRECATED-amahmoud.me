Unitron's History

Unitron was founded in 1964 in Newfoundland, Canada by partners Fred Stork, Rolf Strothmann, and Rolf Dohmer. Unitron’s mission is to provide top of the line hearing aid technology to improve the lives of those who have hearing loss. Since the beginning, Unitron has been focused on products and features that benefit the users directly. Throughout the 1960s-1980s, Unitron became a leader in Behind the Ear (BTE) hearing aids for people with severe to profound hearing loss.

As hearing aids transitioned from analogue to digital, Unitron did not stop innovating. As the 1990s began, Unitron started provided user-friendly and intuitive fitting software. This fitting software allowed advanced adjustments to be done to the digital hearing aids.

In the early 2000s, Unitron was acquired by Phonak AG which eventually was transitioned into Sonova. Unitron continued growing and eventually introduced the FLEX™ ecosystem and FLEX:TRIAL™ which allowed potential users to try the latest technologies before purchase.

Did you know?
In October 2016, Unitron released the smallest wireless RIC hearing aids (Moxi Now) in the world!

Unitron's Mission and Culture

As mentioned above, Unitron’s mission is to provide the best experience to its users. This person-centric culture is embedded in the entirety of the company, from top to bottom. Even as an employee, Unitron goes to large lengths to ensure that a positive personal relationship with the company.

There are many benefits as an employee at Unitron and they continue to add various policies that benefit employees. An example of this is the day-to-day working hours. At Unitron, they allow employees to work within a specific range of hours to either leave earlier or come in later than usual. In addition, they allow employees to work extra hours during the week to leave earlier on another day. These policies add extra flexibility to the workplace and increase morale.

Finally, there are various internal committees (in addition to HR) that ensure a positive internal culture. The first is the Social Committee, they work closely with HR to run various events for employees such as lunches and sporting events. The events are accessible, cheap, and there is no pressure to participate if you are not interested. Unitron also has the Unitron Community Connection which regularly run charity events which give employees an opportunity to contribute to something positive.

Unitron's Software Technology

Unitron offers Truefit™, a desktop hearing aid fitting software that allows hearing healthcare providers to make changes to the hearing aid software. In addition to this, Unitron offers technology levels, were by using Truefit™ a user can easily upgrade a hearing aid by adding more programs. Hearing aid programs are profiles which adjust various frequencies to improve hearing based on the environment (i.e. Driving program or TV program). Truefit™ is built using WPF technology.

In addition to Truefit™ there is the Web and Mobile side of development. The mobile developers produce apps that pair directly with the hearing aids to provide the hearing aid user troubleshooting steps, customization options and much more. The Web side houses the production databases (SQL, CosmosDB and more) and various front-end/back-end features that target hearing healthcare provides and users. Majority of web technology utilized the .NET framework and the user interfaces are build using Angular.

The Patient Insights web app allows healthcare providers to see realtime ratings and comments of the user's hearing aids
